# Introduction

Legal ERP Platform is a matter-centered practice management platform for law
firms and legal service organizations. It brings clients, matters, tasks,
documents, calendar activities, quotations, billing, and financial visibility
into one operating system.

The platform is designed to reduce manual administration, improve accuracy
across client and matter records, and give teams clearer visibility from intake
through delivery and collection. Each matter acts as the primary work container:
users can connect people, documents, assignments, appointments, quotations,
invoices, and profitability signals to the same operational record.

## Who It Serves

- Law firms that need a shared workspace for legal operations
- Legal service teams that manage multiple clients, matters, and documents
- Administrators who need role-based access, tenant configuration, and reporting
- Finance and operations users who need visibility into quotation, billing, and
  collection status

## Core Capabilities

- Client and matter management
- Document automation and centralized document handling
- Task, team, and calendar coordination
- Quotation, billing, and financial visibility
- Role-based administration for users, permissions, and tenant settings
- Reporting foundations for operational and profitability analysis

## Operating Model

Each tenant represents one organization using the platform in an isolated SaaS
environment. Users work through role-based permissions so each team member only
sees and performs the actions appropriate to their responsibility.

The matter record is the center of daily work. Related clients, documents,
tasks, calendar events, quotations, invoices, payments, and performance data
should be traceable from the matter so teams can understand the current status
without searching across disconnected tools.

## Technical Foundation

The accepted implementation stack for the Legal ERP Platform is Django for the
backend, React with Tailwind CSS for the frontend, and PostgreSQL for the
database. Other stack references in source requirement files are treated as
superseded unless a later architecture decision changes this.
